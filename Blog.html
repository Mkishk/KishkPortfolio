Understanding Learning Outcomes - Rhetorical Knowledge

Rhetorical knowledge is the ability to identify and apply strategies across a range of texts and writing situations. To me, this means that the writer would be able to write all types of different literary works. Like knowing the difference and applying the conventions of formal versus informal.

To understand this learning outcome, you have to be able to tell which writing style you need to apply to that certain situation. You must be able to adjust your techniques to the situation and be able to be versatile with your writing. Throughout the semester, we have had many different writing situations and used different writing styles. My EIP is more formal and uses a bunch of multimedia in order to make it interesting to the reader. While my blog posts are very informal and display a more relaxed tone.

This learning outcome is very important to a writer so that you can be able to write in any given situation.

Just by knowing the genre, we will judge whether we like it or not. If you always listen to hip hop and R&B and then somone recommends a country song, you probably wouldnt bother to listen to it because you already know what to expect before listening to the song.

Academic Writing

Irvin's article about academic writing gave a lot of great information about writing in college. The article highlighted some myths that I have been doing my whole life and I realize now that I dont need to write the essay like that. One of the myths that applied to me was the Five Paragraph Essay myth. All throughout highschool, we were told that our essays had to have an introduction with a thesis, 3 supporting body paragraphs and a conclusion. I thought that was just the way to write essays since I have been doing it for the last four years. I now realize that this format doesnt make a great essay and that any format I use will be fine as long as the content is good.

Another myth that I could really relate to was the Never use "I" myth. In highschool, we were always told that in formal essays there should never be first person pronouns because your opinion shouldnt be a part of the essay. In 11th grade we wrote a research paper that took us the whole year and students were docked points if first person pronouns were present. After reading this article, I now know that there is no format or correct way to write an essay and that you should just write it to the best of your ability.

What makes Inquiry Questions Unique?

An inquiry question is unique because its a question that doesnt have a direct answer and should be open to research. You should be able to find some answers to the question through research but it is still a difficult question to answer. What makes these questions unique is that, you really dont know the answer or it may have multiple answers. An inquiry question doesnt have a simple yes or no answer.

Inquiry questions have a clear focus on what they are asking and make invoke different opinions. You may feel one way about the answer to the question but someone else may see it from a different perspective. This makes them really unique because the differing opinions can lead to discussion about the topic.

My Composition Process

When I am writing an essay, I dont really have a set process. Most of the time, I dont prewrite or brainstorm on paper beforehand. I will think about what I want to add and plan it out in my head before I actually start writing. When I start writing, I usually start at the beginning with the introduction, but I do the hook last. I will write the entire intro paragraph and thesis and then after completing that, I will start to think on what I should make my hook. Because to me the hook is one of those parts that can either make or break your essay. If you have a great hook that gets the reader interested from the beginning than they are more open-minded and attentive when reading through your essay, but if you have a terrible, dull, boring hook, it wont really get the reader excited about reading through your essay. I put a lot of effort into thinking of a good hook because I feel that it is an important part of the essay.

After I write my hook and introduction, I will look at my thesis to see how I want to structure my body paragraphs. I will put the body paragraphs in the order they were written in thesis to keep everything in context and easy to follow. When Writing the body paragrahs, I look at all the information I have and see how I want to structure my body paragraphs. I will always write the transition sentence at the beginning of the next paragraph because I feel that it flows more smoothly that way. After finishing the body paragraphs, I will write my conclusion that summarizes everything I talked about. After that, I proofread to check for grammar or spelling errors. Then im done.
